David Klappholz is a veteran computer science educator and Associate Professor at Stevens Institute of Technology, where he has taught since 1984. With more than four decades of academic experience, he is best known for leading senior capstone projects that connect students with real-world clients, including companies, startups, nonprofits, and government agencies. His approach emphasizes practical software engineering skills such as requirements analysis, project management, teamwork, client communication, and software delivery, helping students bridge the gap between academic learning and professional practice. Klappholz's educational background combines a bachelor's degree in humanities, mathematics, and linguistics from Massachusetts Institute of Technology with a master's degree in electrical engineering and computer science from University of Pennsylvania, reflecting both technical depth and interdisciplinary thinking. In addition to his teaching role, he served as a visiting researcher at University of Southern California for several years, demonstrating ongoing engagement with research and the broader academic community. Overall, his career reflects a strong commitment to preparing students for successful software engineering careers through hands-on, industry-focused learning experiences.
