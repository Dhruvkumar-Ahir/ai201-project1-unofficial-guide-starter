Stevens Institute of Technology is a private university founded in 1870 and located in Hoboken, New Jersey, directly across the Hudson River from New York City. According to the information provided, the university is ranked #80 among National Universities in the 2026 U.S. News rankings and is also recognized as one of the most innovative schools in the country. The institution has a relatively small undergraduate population of about 4,200 students, creating a more personalized academic environment with an 11:1 student-to-faculty ratio. The campus covers 55 acres in an urban setting and follows a semester-based academic calendar.

Academically, Stevens is particularly known for its strengths in engineering, computer science, business, mathematics, and technology-related disciplines. Engineering is the most popular major, accounting for 43% of graduates, while computer and information sciences account for 24% of graduates. The university demonstrates strong career outcomes, with computer science graduates reporting particularly high starting salaries. The institution also maintains a strong research profile, publishing thousands of research papers and achieving significant citation impact, indicating that faculty members are actively engaged in producing influential scholarly work.

Admissions to Stevens are moderately selective, with an acceptance rate of 48%. The university operates under a test-optional admissions policy, meaning applicants are not required to submit SAT or ACT scores. However, admitted students generally present strong academic credentials, including SAT scores ranging from 1380 to 1505, ACT scores between 31 and 34, and an average high school GPA of approximately 3.9. These figures suggest that successful applicants typically have excellent academic records and strong preparation for rigorous college-level coursework.

The cost of attendance is substantial, with annual tuition and fees listed at $65,606. Housing and food expenses add approximately $20,000 per year. However, financial aid plays an important role in reducing costs for many students. Fifty-nine percent of first-year students receive need-based financial aid, and the average aid package exceeds $43,000. As a result, the average annual cost after aid for eligible students is significantly lower than the published price.

One of Stevens' strongest selling points is its return on investment. The university reports an impressive 81% four-year graduation rate, which is considerably higher than the national average. Six years after graduation, alumni report a median salary of over $90,000. Student and alumni reviews repeatedly emphasize the school's strong job placement opportunities, practical education, and excellent reputation among employers in engineering, technology, and quantitative fields. Several reviewers specifically mention obtaining employment quickly after graduation and attribute their career success to the problem-solving skills and technical training developed at Stevens.

Campus life combines a technical academic atmosphere with access to the opportunities of the New York metropolitan area. Students participate in more than 55 organizations, 13 fraternities, and 8 sororities. Housing options include coeducational residence halls, women's housing, fraternity housing, and sorority housing. Reviewers frequently praise the campus location, describing Hoboken as safe, vibrant, and filled with restaurants, entertainment, and easy transportation links to New York City through ferries and public transit. Many alumni cite the location as one of the university's greatest advantages.

Overall, Stevens Institute of Technology appears to be a highly regarded institution for students interested in engineering, computer science, technology, business, and quantitative fields. Although the cost of attendance is relatively high, the university's strong academic reputation, high graduation rates, successful employment outcomes, and proximity to New York City contribute to a strong value proposition for many students. Alumni reviews consistently highlight the quality of education, career opportunities, and long-term professional benefits associated with a Stevens degree.
