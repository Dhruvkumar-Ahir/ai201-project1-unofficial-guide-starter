Based on everything you pasted—the Reddit discussions, professor comments, Niche profile, and business-school faculty list—the overall picture is fairly consistent:

Stevens' strengths
Stevens Institute of Technology has a strong STEM reputation, especially in engineering, quantitative finance, data science, and technology-oriented business fields.
Small class sizes (about 10:1 student-faculty ratio according to the Niche page).
Strong job-placement and salary outcomes are frequently highlighted by the school and many students.
Location in Hoboken gives easy access to New York City internships and networking.
Many adjunct faculty come directly from industry (Google, American Express, Citi, Johnson & Johnson, Verizon, HSBC, etc.), which can be valuable for networking and applied coursework.
Common criticisms
Cost is the biggest complaint. Even with aid, many students question whether the premium over a public university is worth it.
Social life is generally viewed as more limited than at a large state school.
The campus is small, and some students feel it lacks the traditional college experience.
Workload is often described as intense and stressful.
Housing, dining, and administrative issues come up repeatedly in reviews.
Rutgers comparison

The comments you pasted largely point toward Rutgers University–New Brunswick if:

You're pursuing IT/ITI specifically.
Cost matters.
You want a larger social scene and more traditional college experience.
You prefer the flexibility and breadth that comes with a much larger university.

The strongest argument for Stevens is usually:

"I specifically want Stevens' program, small-campus environment, NYC proximity, and I'm comfortable paying the additional cost."

The strongest argument for Rutgers is:

"I can get the program I want, save a substantial amount of money, and still have strong career opportunities."

The Stevens data suggests it's a solid school with excellent outcomes, but most of the people comparing the two schools felt that Rutgers offers a better value proposition unless Stevens has a specific program or opportunity that you can't get elsewhere.
